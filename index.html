The Project

The representation of lust has a long history in the art world. In the visual arts, lust has been used as a recurring theme throughout history to represent, and often condemn, the physical and sexual desire. Our project aims at investigating some of the main visual representations of lust in the history of art, from the XVI century to the contemporary era. This work focuses, in the first place, on showing how the iconographic representation of lust chnaged over time, but it also investigates the feminine figure in it and how the concept of woman associated to lust shifted throughout centuries.

Which was the visual representation of the idea of lust in the XVI century? How did it change in the next centuries? Is the figure of the woman associated in a positive or negative way to the vice of lust? Did her sexual desire emancipate over time? These are some of the questions that this project aims at answering, with the visual support of images in high resolution and great details thanks to the employ of IIIF and Mirador.

Lust

Lust is classified by the Christian tradition as one of the seven capital sins. It indicates an excessive, that is irrational, attachment to venereal pleasure. For centuries, the idea of lust has been attributed to the Devil, demons and evil spirits. This is because it was believed that lust led to the breaking of marriage vows, adultery and other immoral acts. In the Christian view, it destroys man's humanity since its purpose is not something recognized by reason, namely the procreation of children and the promotion of the mutual love in marriage, but instead to mere bodily pleasure.

Gender Issue

Gender issues in art are something that has been discussed for decades and will continue to be relevant. Artists continually find new ways to express their opinions on social issues, gender being one of them. Therefore, it comes as no surprise that artists of today also have a keen interest in this topic. Gender has always been presented as an important characteristic in art and continues to be so today. Women have historically been underrepresented in the field of art, and this is something that needs to change, especially when it comes to gender issues in art. For much of history, women were considered secondary to men but, despite their portrayal as the "weaker sex," women have played a powerful role in our history, not only with regard to gender roles, but also in societal norms as a whole A vivid example of gender issues in art can be the representations of women connected to lust. The role woman played during different historical periods was totally changed. The paradigm was shifted over the time. Starting from ancient time lust was seen as heinous sin and woman was usually depicted as wanton and depraved. While now more and more artists see in lust something concealed and depict feminine as the object of beauty and hidden faculty.
